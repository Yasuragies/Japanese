Introduction to Japanese Adjective.
        Introduction to Japanese verb.
        
        Grammar
           In Japanese language, Adjective is devided into 3 different category.
           1. い-adj
           2. な-adj
           3. の-adj
           
           い-adj are the adjectives that ended with い
           
           かわいい = cute
           おいしい = tasty/delicious
           さびしい = lonely
           かなしい = sad
           うれしい = happy
           うるさい = noisy
           やさしい = kind
           忙しい = busy
           暗くらい = dark
           暑あつい = hot (weather)
           熱あつい = hot (object)
           寒さむい = cold
           白しろい = white
           青あおい = blue
           赤あかい = red
           黒くろい = black

           な-adj
           賑にぎやか = lively
           元気げんき = fine/cheerful
           好すき = fond/like

           exception
           嫌い (even tho 嫌い ended with い, this is infact に adjective)

           it is called な adjective because we need to insert な after the adjective before the noun that we want to modify
           ex:

           賑やかな町
           lively town.

           元気な人
           cheerful person.

           好きな曲
           music that (i) like.

           好きな人
           Person that (i) like.

           3. の-adj (almost every noun can be attached with の to turn it into adjective)

           日本語の先生
           Japanese teacher.

           永遠の命
           Eternal life.
